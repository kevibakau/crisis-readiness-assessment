Question 1 of 7Safety & Wellbeing

Are you safe right now?

Physical and emotional safety is the foundation. If you are not safe — nothing else can be addressed until that changes. This is the first question because it is the most important one.

Physically and emotionally — are you safe where you are right now? Do you have shelter, food, and are you free from immediate physical threat or harm?

A
Yes — I Am Safe
I have safe shelter, food, and am not under immediate physical threat. My immediate safety needs are met.
B
Mostly Safe — Some Concerns
I am generally safe but there are concerns about my living situation, food security, or emotional wellbeing that need attention.
C
Unstable — Safety Is At Risk
My housing, food, or physical safety is unstable or threatened. I am not confident I can maintain safety without help.
D
No — I Am Not Safe
I am in immediate danger or do not have safe shelter, adequate food, or am experiencing threats to my physical safety.
If you are in immediate danger, please contact emergency services (000 in Australia, 911 in US) or a crisis hotline immediately. Lifeline Australia: 13 11 14.
Question 2 of 7Therapeutic & Emotional Support

Do you have professional help managing this?

Therapeutic support in a crisis is not just counseling. It is learning new skills under pressure — nervous system regulation, attachment theory, communication in conflict, co-parenting frameworks. You need someone who can teach you these tools now, not six months from now.

Do you currently have professional therapeutic support — a therapist, counselor, or psychologist who understands your situation and is actively helping you develop the skills to manage it?

A
Yes — Active and Effective
I have regular sessions with a professional who understands my situation and is helping me develop practical skills to manage it.
B
Yes — But Not Crisis-Focused
I have a therapist but they are not equipped for crisis-focused work or teaching the specific skills I need right now.
C
Searching — No Support Yet
I know I need help and am looking, but have not yet found the right professional or started regular sessions.
D
No — Managing Alone
I do not have professional therapeutic support and am managing the emotional weight of this crisis entirely on my own.
Managing a crisis alone without therapeutic support significantly increases emotional, relational, and decision-making costs. Professional help is not a luxury — it is foundational.
Question 3 of 7Financial & Government Assistance

Can you absorb the financial impact?

Personal crises carry immediate costs — legal fees, moving expenses, therapy, lost income. If you cannot absorb these costs, government assistance programs exist that most people do not know how to access. You need someone who knows the system.

Can you currently absorb the financial costs of what is happening — legal fees, housing, therapy, lost income — and do you know what government assistance or support programs you may qualify for?

A
Yes — Financially Stable
I can absorb the costs without threatening my ability to meet basic needs. My financial situation is stable.
B
Manageable — With Difficulty
I can cover costs but it is creating significant financial strain. I may need assistance or better understanding of what support is available.
C
Struggling — Need Assistance
I cannot absorb these costs without help. I do not know what government assistance or programs I qualify for or how to access them.
D
Crisis — Immediate Financial Threat
I am facing immediate financial crisis. I cannot pay for housing, legal help, or basic needs without emergency assistance.
Government assistance programs — welfare, legal aid, housing support, crisis payments — are difficult to navigate. You need someone who knows what you qualify for and how to access it.
Question 4 of 7Legal

Do you have legal representation?

Legal decisions made under crisis pressure without proper representation cost more than you can afford to lose. Mediation versus family court. Property settlement. Custody arrangements. You need someone who knows the system and can protect your interests.

Do you currently have legal representation who understands your situation, and do you understand your legal obligations and options — including mediation versus court processes?

A
Yes — Represented and Informed
I have legal representation. I understand my obligations, options, and the difference between mediation and court processes.
B
Partial — Some Advice, Not Full Representation
I have received some legal advice but do not have ongoing representation or full clarity on my options and obligations.
C
Searching — No Representation Yet
I know I need legal help but have not yet secured representation. I do not fully understand my legal position or options.
D
No — Managing Legal Decisions Alone
I do not have legal representation and am making legal decisions without professional guidance. I do not know what I am entitled to or obligated to do.
Legal decisions without representation in crisis situations consistently produce worse financial and relational outcomes. Legal aid exists for those who cannot afford private counsel.
Question 5 of 7Employment & Income

Is your income secure?

Personal crises affect your ability to work. If you lose your job or cannot maintain performance under pressure, you need to understand your employment protections, disability options, and how to maintain income continuity while managing the crisis.

Is your employment and income currently stable, and if you are at risk of losing work or have already lost it — do you understand your employment rights, disability options, and how to maintain income?

A
Secure — Income Is Stable
My employment is secure and my ability to work is not threatened by what I am going through. Income is stable.
B
At Risk — Performance or Attendance Affected
My work performance or attendance is being affected. I am concerned about job security but have not yet sought help or understanding of my rights.
C
Unstable — Already Lost Work or Reduced Hours
I have lost work, had hours reduced, or am on leave. I do not fully understand my options for maintaining income or my employment protections.
D
No Income — Unemployed Without Support
I have no current income and do not know how to access unemployment benefits, disability support, or emergency financial assistance.
Employment law protects workers experiencing personal crisis in many situations. You may be entitled to leave, accommodations, or protection from termination that you do not know about.
Question 6 of 7Administrative & Daily Operations

Can you manage daily operations?

When you are in crisis you lose the ability to manage the basic operations of your life. Bills. Appointments. Insurance. Household management. If you were not the administrative partner, you may not know what is due when or where the documents are. Someone needs to help you build this.

Are you currently able to manage daily administrative tasks — paying bills, attending appointments, managing household operations, keeping track of important documents and deadlines?

A
Yes — Fully Managing
I am keeping up with bills, appointments, and household operations. I know what is due when and can manage administrative tasks effectively.
B
Struggling — Some Tasks Slipping
I am managing most things but some tasks are slipping. I am concerned about missing deadlines or losing track of important obligations.
C
Overwhelmed — Cannot Keep Up
I cannot keep up with daily administrative tasks. Bills may be going unpaid, appointments missed, and I do not have a clear system for tracking what needs to be done.
D
No System — Completely Lost
I have no system for managing household operations. I do not know what bills are due, what appointments exist, or where important documents are.
Administrative failure in a crisis leads to missed deadlines, unpaid bills, and additional legal and financial consequences. Someone needs to sit with you and build this infrastructure.
Question 7 of 7Support & Coordination

Who is sitting with you through this?

This is the war room question for individuals. Do you have someone who sees all of it — not just their piece? Someone coordinating your therapeutic, legal, financial, employment, and administrative needs simultaneously? Or are you managing all of this alone while also trying to survive emotionally?

Do you have committed daily support — friends, family, clergy, or a professional coordinator who is sitting with you through this and helping you manage all domains simultaneously?

A
Yes — Daily Committed Support
I have people who check in daily, sit with me through difficult moments, and help me coordinate across all the domains I am managing.
B
Some Support — But Not Daily or Coordinated
I have people who care, but nobody is sitting with me daily or helping coordinate across all the domains. Support is fragmented.
C
Limited — Mostly Managing Alone
I have minimal support. Most days I am managing this entirely on my own with nobody to help coordinate or provide emotional presence.
D
No — Completely Alone
I am going through this completely alone. I have nobody who is sitting with me daily, and I am coordinating everything by myself.
Going through crisis alone is the single greatest predictor of poor outcomes — financially, emotionally, and relationally. You need people sitting with you. This is not optional.

Nobody should be managing all of this by themselves. The support you need exists — but most people do not know what to ask for or where to find it. Here is what matters most:
Therapeutic support immediately — weekly minimum, daily check-ins if the crisis is acute. Someone teaching you nervous system regulation, attachment frameworks, communication in conflict.
